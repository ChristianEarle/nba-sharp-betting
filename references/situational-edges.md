# Situational Edges — NBA Schedule & Context Analysis

Read this file when evaluating a specific game or schedule spot for clawdbot.
These are the most reliable, research-backed situational factors in NBA betting.

---

## 1. BACK-TO-BACK (B2B) GAMES

The single most reliable situational edge in NBA betting. Teams average ~14.9 B2B sets
per season (down 23% from a decade ago), but the impact remains significant.

### Impact by Situation

| B2B Scenario | Estimated Impact | Edge Reliability |
|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Road B2B (2nd game away) | -3 to -4 points offensive efficiency | **Highest** |
| Road-to-road B2B | -2.5 to -3.5 points | **High** |
| Home B2B (2nd game at home) | -1 to -2 points | **Moderate** |
| B2B vs. rested opponent (3+ days) | -3 to -5 points net swing | **Highest** |
| B2B after OT game | Additional -1 to -2 points | **High** |

### B2B Analysis Checklist

1. **Is either team on the 2nd of a B2B?** → If yes, this is a primary edge factor
2. **Travel distance between B2B games?** → Cross-country (e.g., NYC to LAL) amplifies fatigue
3. **Time zones crossed?** → East-to-West travel is less disruptive than West-to-East
4. **Did the 1st game go to OT?** → Extra minutes compound fatigue
5. **Rest advantage differential?** → The bigger the gap, the bigger the edge
6. **Which players absorb heavy minutes?** → Guards (5+ miles per game) decline most on B2B

### Position-Specific B2B Impact (for Props)

- **Guards**: Scoring and assists decline most. They cover the most distance through
  constant movement, defensive rotations, and transition running. B2B guard props
  (especially scoring) show the most consistent decline.
- **Forwards**: Moderate decline in scoring, rebounds relatively stable.
- **Centers**: Minutes may decrease but per-minute production stays steadiest.
  Rebounds are the most B2B-resilient stat.

### Star Rest Management

Modern NBA teams aggressively rest stars on B2Bs, especially:
- During the regular season when playoff seeding is comfortable
- On road B2Bs in the 2nd game
- When the next game is against a conference rival or playoff opponent
- Players on minutes restrictions or coming back from injury

**This creates a secondary edge**: the market prices the game assuming star plays,
then the team announces rest → line moves sharply. If clawdbot monitors injury
reports faster than Kalshi reprices, there's a trade.

## 2. REST ADVANTAGE / DISADVANTAGE

### Rest Days Impact Table

| Your Team Rest | Opponent Rest | Net Advantage | Typical ATS Edge |
|---------------|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| 3+ days | B2B | +4 to +6 points | Strong |
| 3+ days | 1 day rest | +2 to +3 points | Moderate |
| 2 days | B2B | +2 to +4 points | Moderate-Strong |
| 2 days | 1 day rest | +1 to +2 points | Slight |
| Equal rest | Equal rest | Neutral | None |
| B2B | 3+ days | -4 to -6 points | Strong fade |

### Rust Factor (Too Much Rest)

Teams with 4+ days off sometimes show "rust" — particularly on offense in the first half.
This is a less reliable edge than fatigue but worth noting:
- First-half unders can be valuable when both teams have excessive rest
- This effect is more pronounced early in the season

## 3. TRAVEL AND SCHEDULING SPOTS

### High-Value Schedule Spots

**The Letdown Spot**
- Team just beat a top-5 opponent or rival → plays a bottom-10 team next
- Emotional and focus letdown → underperformance as small favorites
- Look for value on the underdog or under in these spots

**The Trap Game**
- Team has a marquee matchup coming up (rivalry, national TV) in 2-3 days
- Current game is against a non-threatening opponent
- Team may subconsciously look ahead → flat performance

**The Revenge Game**
- Team faces opponent that beat them badly recently (15+ point loss)
- Especially potent when the rematch is at home
- Players are motivated, coach has prepared specific adjustments
- This is a public-leaning angle though — only play if the price is right

**The West Coast Swing (for Eastern Conference teams)**
- 3+ game road trip through the Western time zones
- Performance degrades as the trip extends, especially games 3+
- West Coast late starts (10:30 PM ET tip) are particularly rough
- Under and underdog value increases as the trip extends

**The Long Homestand Return**
- Team returns from a 5+ game road trip to play at home
- First game back often shows emotional boost but physical fatigue
- Subsequent home games show improvement — look for value on games 2-3 of homestand

### Travel Distance Factors

| Route Type | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Same city / short hop (<500 mi) | Negligible |
| Regional (500-1000 mi) | Minor — factor only if B2B |
| Cross-country (1000-2500 mi) | Significant — 1-2 point impact |
| Cross-country + time zone change | Major — 2-3 point impact |
| International (Toronto) + customs | Add small friction factor |

## 4. HOME COURT ADVANTAGE

### The Baseline

NBA home court advantage has compressed over the past decade:
- Historical average: ~3.5 points
- Current era: ~2.5 to 3.0 points
- Varies significantly by team and venue

### Teams With Elevated Home Court

Some arenas create a measurably stronger home advantage:
- High altitude (Denver — altitude genuinely affects visiting teams, especially in 4th quarter)
- Elite fan environments (certain playoff atmospheres)
- Teams with significant home/away performance splits

### Home Court in Fourth Quarters

Research shows home court advantage **amplifies in close fourth quarters**:
- Pace slows, pressure increases
- Referee calls show measurable home bias in close games
- This matters for live betting and props targeting 4th quarter performance

## 5. NATIONALLY TELEVISED GAME EFFECTS

### The Public Bias Amplifier

National TV games (ESPN, TNT, ABC) draw disproportionate public betting action:
- Favorites get overbet more heavily than in non-televised games
- Line inflation on favorites: typically 0.5-1.5 points
- This creates systematic value on underdogs in nationally televised games

### The Motivation Factor

Some players/teams demonstrably elevate for national TV:
- Star players with high usage rates tend to be more aggressive
- This can affect player props (overs on scoring for "showtime" players)
- Teams fighting for playoff positioning also elevate on national stage

**Trade this carefully** — the public also bets this angle, partially pricing it in.

## 6. SEASONAL CONTEXT

### Early Season (Oct-Nov)
- Teams are still gelling — recent performance is less predictive
- New player acquisitions haven't fully integrated
- **Edge**: Power ratings based on preseason projections + roster talent > early results

### Mid-Season (Dec-Feb)
- Sample sizes become meaningful (~30+ games)
- Models start becoming reliable
- **Edge**: Schedule analysis is most valuable — teams are tired, B2B impact peaks

### Pre-All-Star (Jan-Feb)
- Teams with comfortable playoff positions may coast
- Teams on the bubble play with urgency
- **Edge**: Motivation differentials are large and often mispriced

### Post-All-Star (Feb-Apr)
- Teams with locked playoff spots rest stars more aggressively
- Tanking teams become unreliable — they may try hard or not at all
- **Edge**: Focus on teams with clear motivation (fighting for seeding, play-in spots)

### Playoffs
- Home court advantage increases significantly in playoffs
- Star players play more minutes — fatigue becomes less of a factor for stars
  but more for role players
- **Edge**: Role player performance variance increases, creating prop opportunities

## 7. REFEREE ANALYSIS

An often-overlooked situational factor. Different referee crews have measurable tendencies:

### What to Track

- **Foul calls per game**: Some crews call significantly more fouls → affects pace and totals
- **Home team foul differential**: Some refs show stronger home bias
- **Over/Under tendency**: High-foul refs correlate with higher-scoring games (more FT attempts)

### Where to Get Ref Data

- NBA.com official referee assignments (released morning of game day)
- Basketball Reference referee stats page
- Specialty sites that track ref tendencies by game

### How to Use It

This is a **secondary edge** — it should confirm or add to an existing thesis, not be
the primary reason for a trade. Example: you already like the under based on B2B +
defensive matchup, and the assigned ref crew averages 2.5 fewer fouls than league average.
That's confluence.

## 8. COMBINING SITUATIONAL FACTORS

The most powerful edges come from **stacking multiple situational factors** that all
point in the same direction. clawdbot should score games on a confluence scale:

### Confluence Scoring

| # of Aligned Factors | Confidence Level | Sizing Multiplier |
|---------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1 factor | Low — needs strong model edge | 0.5x normal size |
| 2 factors | Moderate | 1.0x normal size |
| 3+ factors | High | 1.5x normal size (cap at 3% bankroll) |

### Example: High-Confluence Spot

**Game: Lakers @ Celtics, Tuesday night**
- ✅ Lakers on 2nd of B2B (played in Denver Monday)
- ✅ Cross-country travel (Denver → Boston = 1,800 mi + 2 time zones)
- ✅ Celtics on 3 days rest
- ✅ Nationally televised → public overbet on the name-brand matchup
- ✅ Ref crew averages fewer fouls than league average → lower pace → under value

This is a 5-factor confluence spot. If the Kalshi price on Celtics YES is ≤75¢
and your model says 82%, this is a max-confidence trade (3% bankroll).

The under also has strong confluence if the total is priced above where the
B2B + travel + pace factors project.
